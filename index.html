Overview

OVERVIEW OF THE FINAL PORTFOLIO FORMAT:
The format I have chosen for the portfolio is a website. Since I have experience creating a webpage using HTML and CSS, I figured it would be a great way to combine my interest for web development with this biology portfolio project. The website's homepage has a picture of each of the 9 learning outcomes from BI 102. The image represents the learning outcome, and under the picture is the name of the outcome. When you click on a learning outcome, then a pop up page will appear with examples that include a variety of media assignments and/or quiz answers.

HOW THE PORTFOLIO DEMONSTRATES MASTERY OF THE COURSE OUTCOMES:
Each media piece in the portfolio is an excellent representation of my understanding of a learning outcome. The portfolio demonstrates mastery of the course outcomes because it includes media assignments and quiz answers that have received full credit and were recommended by TAs to include them in my portfolio. This way, I have ensured that my best work pieces are in the portfolio. In addition, I revised some media assignments and quiz answers that I did not get full credit on, and improved them based on the feedback I received by the TAs.

HOW THE PORTFOLIO MAY BE USED IN THE FUTURE:
I figured having a website would allow me to easily share the learning outcomes with family and friends. It also allows them to share it with their friends and so on. This way, the content and knowledge will continue to be shared beyond this class! In the future, the portfolio can also be updated easily and updated information can be added. Studying & handling animals

Photo by pouria oskuie on Unsplash

Common name: Goldfish
Scientific name: Carassius auratus
Items you can feed your goldfish:

  • Flake foods
  • Earthworms
  • Bloodworms
  • Brine Shrimp
  • Peas
  • Kale
  • Lettuce

At home or in captivity, they should be fed on a regular basis, about 2-3 times a day in order to supply them with the adequate amount of energy and nutrients to survive. Since they are omnivores, they eat meat and plants. You can buy food for them at a local pet store for a reasonable price to keep them energized and healthy. Similar to us, we wouldnâ€™t want to eat the same thing every day so, instead of purchasing and feeding the fish the same food all the time, it is nice to switch it around and feed your goldfish different types of food. On a regular basis, you should feed goldfish some dry food, which are sold in cans or small bottles. To feed the fish, simply put a dash of the recommended portion size inside the fish tank. The food will stay at the very top and the fish will swim and eat the flakes.

If you decide to feed the goldfish live food such as bloodworms, it is recommended that you buy it from a pet store, rather than get it from your backyard. This is an important precaution in order to limit the spread of disease to the fish. You can also feed your fish fruit and vegetables, which allow them to get a good amount of nutrients and they are also healthy. Before you feed your fish fruits or vegetables, be sure to wash them and cut them in small pieces, in order for them to digest them easily. Although it can be fun to feed the fish, be sure to not overfeed them. A good rule of thumb is to feed them an amount that they can digest under two minutes. By taking these dietary precautions and feeding them on a regular basis, you can increase their life-span and keep them as a pet for longer periods of time!
