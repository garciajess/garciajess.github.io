A multicolor flower can be caused by anthocyanins or carotenoids, which give plants their color. In this case, it looks like it was mainly caused by carotenoids, which are responsible for yellow and red pigments. The temperature and environment the plants are in can also play a role in how pigmented they are.

Photo by pouria oskuie on Unsplash

Common name: Goldfish
Scientific name: Carassius auratus
Items you can feed your goldfish:

  • Flake foods
  • Earthworms
  • Bloodworms
  • Brine Shrimp
  • Peas
  • Kale
  • Lettuce

At home or in captivity, they should be fed on a regular basis, about 2-3 times a day in order to supply them with the adequate amount of energy and nutrients to survive. Since they are omnivores, they eat meat and plants. You can buy food for them at a local pet store for a reasonable price to keep them energized and healthy. Similar to us, we wouldn’t want to eat the same thing every day so, instead of purchasing and feeding the fish the same food all the time, it is nice to switch it around and feed your goldfish different types of food. On a regular basis, you should feed goldfish some dry food, which are sold in cans or small bottles. To feed the fish, simply put a dash of the recommended portion size inside the fish tank. The food will stay at the very top and the fish will swim and eat the flakes.

If you decide to feed the goldfish live food such as bloodworms, it is recommended that you buy it from a pet store, rather than get it from your backyard. This is an important precaution in order to limit the spread of disease to the fish. You can also feed your fish fruit and vegetables, which allow them to get a good amount of nutrients and they are also healthy. Before you feed your fish fruits or vegetables, be sure to wash them and cut them in small pieces, in order for them to digest them easily. Although it can be fun to feed the fish, be sure to not overfeed them. A good rule of thumb is to feed them an amount that they can digest under two minutes. By taking these dietary precautions and feeding them on a regular basis, you can increase their life-span and keep them as a pet for longer periods of time!
